Famous Leaders and Their Speaking Styles

Steve Jobs (2015)

The story of the truly triumphant success of Steve Jobs and his huge corporation, which turned the concept of technological achievements upside down, giving the world unprecedented opportunities, products and technologies. Steve Jobs' passion for innovation, combined with his skilful use of linguistic strategies, allows us to explore the mechanisms of his influence on his audience

Key trait: Simplicity and suspense.

The Wolf of Wall Street

The story of the meteoric rise of Jordan Belfort and his high-risk brokerage empire unveils the darker side of financial ambition, where charisma and linguistic manipulation become powerful instruments of control. Belfort’s unrestrained drive for wealth, combined with his masterful use of aggressive and persuasive speech strategies, allows us to examine the mechanisms of his influence on both his employees and the audience.

Key trait: Visionary appeal.

Oprah Winfrey

Talk show host and philanthropist. Oprah is a master of pathos — connecting through empathy, storytelling, and personal experience. Her ability to make audiences feel seen and heard is unmatched.

Key trait: Emotional connection.
